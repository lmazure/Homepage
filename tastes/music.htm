Some of my favorites LPs:
Machine Head  The More Things Change ...  I was thinking heavy metal was dying: the heirs of Metallica, Iron Maiden, AC/DC ... were playing noisy music without any rage or soul (I am not a big fan of death metal, black metal...). 

Then I discovered Korn and Machine Head. What a nice surprise! They have taken their predecessors' heritage and pushed it one step further: a special rhythmic and voice for Korn; Machine Head has found the solution to be even more powerful and still be musical (this is not the noise of death metal). 

That's real violence for our minds. 

Let's hope some new bands will continue in this direction. 
Korn  Life Is Peachy 
Nine Inch Nails  The Downward Spiral  While I was missing new heavy metal stuff, I have met industrial music and related styles (techno thrash ...). 

Bands like Fear Factory, Treponem Pal ... are good, but I am the most interested in the new directions allowed by machine generated sounds. In my mind, Nine Inch Nails is simply the best, this is innovative, diverse, sad... 
Further Down The Spiral 
Manuel De Falla  El Amor Brujo  Modern music derives from a mix of African tribal rhythms, European classical music... I do not know a lot of things about the music history (and I regret this ignorance). Being educated in the occidental culture, my brain has been pre-cabled to appreciate classical music. And this is a good thing: there are so many chefs d'œuvre. So let's discover Beethoven, Bach, Spanish composers... 
Samuel Barber  Adagio For Strings 
Sade  Promise  What is the instrument I prefer to listen to? Guitar, violin or piano? 

Nope. I simply prefer the human voice and, more precisely, women voice (perhaps this is because I am male). 
Tori Amos  Boy For Pele 
Trust  Europe et Haine  In some case, melody is not the most important feature in a song. The lyrics message is the key. This is especially true for some hardcore/heavy metal bands (e.g. Rage Against The Machine). 

On the French stage, you can find Trust, Lofofora, No One Is Innocent ... You agree or disagree with their opinions (for example, I strongly disagree with Trust's views on Mesrine), but these guys have an opinion and they are transmitting their messages. 

There is always an itching feel in my mind when I go to this kind of gig. I believe that too many persons just listen to the music and do not take care of the lyrics. So you can see a whole crowd singing a chorus, but I am not sure that all these guys understand the message ... 
Lofofora  Lofofora 
Apocalyptica  Apocalyptica Plays Metallica By Four Cellos  There have been many classical persons playing heavy metal compositions: Led Zeppelin and Deep Purple are the two most well known examples. 

If you think that heavy metal is garbage, you should listen to Apocalyptica. I have already performed the experiment: I have some friends who are keen on classical music, I lent them this LP and their answers were always the same "The music is very well written, what a pity that Metallica uses electric guitars instead of cellos!" 
Pearl Jam  Ten  This LP arrived out of the blue. Nobody knew them before. Now they are one of the most well known bands on earth. This is deserved. I consider Ten as one of the best rock LP ever produced.
FFF  1996 LP (no title)  Some bands are pretty good when you listen to them in your living room. Some are even better on stage. 

This is the case of FFF: if you have never been to one of their concerts, you have never seen a real gig. 
Nirvana  Unplugged In New York  Still missing Kurt Cobain... 
Jeff Buckley  Sketches For "My Sweetheart The Drunk"  Another great singer and composer.

I am surprised that you are still reading this logorrhea.

Perhaps are you taking interest in other people's music tastes and now you are asking yourself if I have real opinions since I am listening to Rage Against The Machine, Nine Inch Nails and Manuel De Falla.

Yes, I have. I do like any music that gives you some feelings, any music with a soul. That's why I dislike most of French variété (by the way, US country music is even worse), easy listening... I regret to not know jazz; I would like to discover more things in classical music; I am interested in some experiments of tribal music integration (have you listened to Sepultura's Roots?); more generally, I am interested in fusion between different music types (I believe that crossbreeding is richness)...

So, may I give you an advice? Do not limit your musical knowledge to what you can ear on the radio. You must be curious. There are a lot of new pearls every day and there are much more old gems already existing. Do not let radio and TV drive your tastes.
