banana-fruit breads

makes two 1 kg loaf tins

  • 285 g fine-milled wholemeal pastry flour
  • 225 g unbleached white flour
  • 2 teaspoonfuls bicarbonate of soda
  • 225 g butter, softened
  • 340 g honey
  • 3 eggs
  • 4 large ripe bananas, mashed
  • 130 g dried apricots, chopped
  • 130 g stoned dates, chopped
  • 85 g walnuts, chopped (optional)

    •  
  • Preheat the oven to 180°C. Grease two 1 kg loaf tins. Line the bases with thin white writing paper. Butter the paper.
  • Sift together the flours and bicarbonate of soda into a medium-sized bowl.
  • Cream the butter in a large bowl until it is light and fluffy. Beat in it the honey, then the eggs, then the bananas. Stir in the dry ingredients. Fold in the fruits and nuts, if desired.
  • Turn the batter in the prepared tins. Bake for 50 minutes, or until a cake tester or wooden cocktail stick inserted in the centre comes out clean and dry. (The breads should rise and split at the top before testing.)
  • Cool for 5 minutes in the tins. Remove the breads from the tins and peel off the paper. Turn out onto wire racks and cool completely.
  • When the breads are completely cold, wrap with aluminium foil. Refrigerate until ready to use.

source: "Sensational Desserts" by Joan Bingham and Dolores Riccio - page 175
